The Commandment Word
Our fathers and grandfathers worked and struggled with all their might to establish for us a free and dignified homeland in which we live in safety and security. Throughout their lives, they were cooperating, understanding, working as one for one goal, which is to serve Kuwait and its people. Thus, they laid a solid foundation for us and wrote a deeply rooted policy for us, which is to always and forever be cooperating and understanding, working as one family, and putting the interest of Kuwait and its people above any other consideration. In this spirit, we, the children of this generation, follow in the footsteps of our fathers and grandfathers, to push Kuwait forward towards new horizons of advancement and progress in all fields.
"Sabah Salem Al SABAH"

Achievements made during his tenure His Highness, may God rest his soul, was keen to confirm that Kuwait is not just an oil-rich country, but in the first place men of responsibility, and based on this awareness and his long experience through assuming many positions, the industry of men and building the nation was his main concern, and it was completed during his reign:

education:
Increasing the number of schools to 229 schools for boys and girls in various stages. - Opening of Kuwait University on November 26, 1966 - Study starting at the College of Medicine in 76/77 - Studying at the College of Engineering and Petroleum in the year 75/76 - Opening of a house for laborers and another for female teachers 68/69 - Opening of special education institutes 1970 Establishment of the Higher Institute for Industrial Education .
the health:
Opening of Al-Midan Hospital 1967 - New Maternity Hospital 1968 - Preparation of designs for the two projects of Mubarak Al-Kabeer Hospital and Al-Adan Hospital in Fahaheel. Increasing numbers of clinics and public health centers - intensifying care in the Nursing Institute.
Economy:
Diversification of national sources of income, so he encouraged the establishment of a non-oil industry, the development of the agricultural and fishing sectors, and in the oil sector, he made Kuwait one of the three founding members of the Organization of Arab Petroleum Exporting Countries (OAPEC) in June 1968, and in August 1974 he established a Supreme Petroleum Council to reorganize the Kuwaiti sector in preparation for the handover of the state His (75-77) established the Central Bank (April 1969) and encouraged the opening of new commercial banks.
Radio and television:
In 1973, television achieved some achievements. The television began broadcasting in color in March 1973, and among its events was the third Arab Gulf Football Tournament, and the radio began sending a program in the Urdu language, and equipped the Radio and Television Complex, which is the unique building of its kind in the region.
Insurances:
The application of social insurances to Kuwaitis, and thus the insurance umbrella extended to all Kuwaiti families, and an Emiri decree was issued by the Future Generations Reserve Law No. 106 of 1976, which provides for an annual deduction of the state’s public revenues (10%) and opens a special account to form a reserve for oil wealth in the name of the future generations reserve.
Army:
In May 1968, His Highness canceled the defense agreement that had been signed with Britain in 1961 - reorganized the Kuwaiti armed forces - established a military academy after the 1967 war and the type of arms sources.
Globally:
During his reign, Kuwait, may God have mercy on him, joined the Non-Aligned Movement, helped the developing countries to solve their problems, demanded from the pulpit of the United Nations the implementation of the resolutions of the international organization, and within the efforts of the Pakistan Friendship Society, a special hall was established in Pakistan, bearing the name of the late Sheikh Al-Salem in honor of his memory and recognition of his constructive efforts internationally .
Arabic:
In the war of 67 he sent a Kuwaiti unit to the Suez Canal and participated in the battles of this war, and in 73 he sent another unit to the Syrian front - he applied the slogan of oil for the battle - he provided generous financial aid to the schools of Aden, Hadhramaut and Mahra and other aid to the Arab countries and in recognition of his thanks and appreciation for his generous humanitarian gesture, Egypt named him Karim on the largest indoor sports hall in the Middle East, which is the Sabah Al-Salem Al-Sabah Hall at the Egyptian Police Academy. And the Medical Education Center at Al-Hussein Teaching Hospital (Wing of Al-Azhar College of Medicine).
