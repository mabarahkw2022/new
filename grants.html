Grants Requirements

Scholarship Terms:
The regulation includes several conditions for admission to scholarships offered by the foundation of the late Sheikh Sabah Al-Salem Al-Mubarak Al-Sabah. The Sabah Al-Salem Al-Mubarak Al-Sabah Foundation offers a limited number of scholarships every year to those admitted to distinguished universities at the bachelor’s and master’s levels for outstanding students from several countries who do not have other means to finance their studies, in order to develop effective scientists and leaders and prepare them for employment and service to advanced people. .
 Scholarships are awarded annually through a competitive application process.
Al-Mabarrah helps students with tuition fees only and does not include living expenses.
Al-Mabarah gives priority to applicants at the bachelor’s level, with priority given to professional studies, and considers applications from distinguished applicants. To obtain a master's degree and scientific research projects.
 Applications for admission to short-term courses are not considered.
 Candidates are evaluated based on their presentation of well-thought-out and coherent educational and career plans, and their interests and achievements outside the curriculum. The possibility of achieving their goals and the likelihood of success in a competitive academic environment.
 They are interviewed by the Grants Committee to assess their financial status, academic performance, extra-curricular achievements and practical plans.
First: Definition of scholarships:

The scholarship is a specific amount of money for a period of one academic year only, the value of which is decided by the Board of Directors, and the scholarship can be renewed based on criteria set by the Foundation’s management and approved by the Board of Directors.

Second: Types of scholarships offered according to the academic degree:
1. Bachelors The first university degree that follows high school, and its study is usually 4 years or a specified number of hours, depending on the specialization and the country.
2. Masters It is a post-bachelor's degree, which is an academic postgraduate degree for advanced university studies. The master's degree is granted after a specific study period, which usually ranges from one to two years, at the end of which a research is required according to the system of the university granting that certificate.
3. Research It concerns specialized studies and the preparation of scientific research and papers capable of scientific publication in scientifically refereed journals. Third: Conditions for applying for the scholarship: Each grant has its own conditions and requirements such as certificates, papers and official documents. These conditions are divided into general conditions and special conditions according to the following: General Terms:
1. The applicant should be of good conduct and behaviour.
2. The age of the scholarship applicant for a bachelor’s degree should not be more than 22 years old, and the master’s student should not be more than 30 years old.
3. The applicant should not have received financial support from any public or private entity, or obtained a scholarship or study mission, in a previous or current period, and then withdrew or was dismissed from it.
4. The applicant must fulfill the conditions set by the donor country.
Submit all the documents required to apply for grants, which are:
1. Identification papers of a civil card - a copy of the passport
2. The official documents attested for the academic stages passed.
3. Transcripts for the previous educational stages.
4. A high school diploma.
5. Personal statement and curriculum vitae.
Special conditions:

1. The applicant for the scholarship must have at least a bachelor’s degree with a general average of 3.50 out of 4.00 or 90% for the secondary stage.
2. Pass the SAT test with no less than 1400 points, or the TOEFL test with no less than 600 points.
3. Obtaining acceptance from one of the distinguished universities recognized by the Ministry of Higher Education in the State of Kuwait.
4. To renew the annual scholarship, the student’s cumulative GPA must not be less than 3.00 out of 4.00.


Important notes:
 Submission or nomination for the scholarship does not mean final acceptance.
 The board of directors of the foundation has full discretionary power to choose and accept candidates and reserves the right to refuse.
 The Board of Directors of the Foundation decides the amount of scholarship funds provided to accepted students on a case-by-case basis.
 Priority in obtaining grants shall be given to applicants for studies in vocational colleges.
 The Foundation’s Board of Directors has the right to exempt the applicant from the aforementioned conditions as it deems appropriate.
