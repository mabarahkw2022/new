Biography
* Sheikh Sabah Al-Salem Al-Mubarak Al-Sabah, the twelfth ruler of Kuwait in the ruling Al-Sabah family, took power on November 24, 1965 until he died on December 31, 1977. Sheikh Sabah Al-Salem was born on November 28, 1915, and his education proceeded according to strict traditional curricula, where teachers enabled him to master the Holy Qur’an, Arabic language and literature. His character when he became a prince. His Arab policy was based on mixing realism and foreseeing a positive and interactive role for Kuwait in Arab economic and social development. He was the first Kuwaiti leader (prince) to visit the White House in Washington and the first Kuwaiti ruler to make a big tour in the Gulf region. He respected the constitution and made every effort to develop and develop Kuwait economically, politically and culturally. He was successful and the father of his family and the beloved Kuwaiti people.
*The Father Prince:-
Sheikh Sabah Al-Salem Al-Sabah was a family man who never neglected his own family. He dined with his family members, discussed daily political issues with them, and was delighted to hear their opinions. He found comfort in the warm embrace of his family and was happy in the company of his children and generous family, devoting himself to the fulfillment of his duties towards the state, his family and the people. at the same time. Because of his humble nature, he was very close to the people and keen to extend and strengthen contact with his people.
His achievements:
At the local level, there were three major projects that took priority during his rule and were among his main interests:
1- Kuwait University, which was initially proposed as an idea when he was appointed as Deputy Prime Minister in 1962 and his dream was to establish a modern university in which both the English and Egyptian education systems would meet. The university was opened in November 1966, where he delivered a speech on the opening day expressing He expressed his vision for the role that this university can play in Kuwaiti life, as he announced (that Kuwait University, which we celebrate its inauguration today, is nothing but a gift offered by the government to its loyal people, and we are fully aware that universities all over the world are nothing but springs of knowledge and factories for the production of researchers and scientists . As part of his affirmation of his continuous association with the university’s issues, he was keen to participate in the celebrations held annually to distribute awards and certificates to graduates. He also distributed to his sons (Ali - Hessa - and Sheikha) to leave their colleges in England and join Kuwait University in their beloved country.
2- Establishing a housing city for Kuwaitis with limited income by providing them with housing units equipped with a complete infrastructure including schools, clinics, police stations, mosques and public parks.
3- As for the third major project that is closest to his heart, it is the establishment of the foundation of Sheikh Sabah Al-Salem Al-Sabah. On January 4, 1976, the Emir issued a decree renewing the foundations necessary to establish We set up the charity and outline its goals. The prince’s motives were disclosed in the preamble to the decree, which confirmed his approval (what God has bestowed upon us) and indicated that the establishment of the charity comes “in line with our conviction that the needy have a right to our wealth, and as a gesture to express our encouragement for outstanding students to continue their higher and university studies, And to support our artists and writers and give them the opportunity to be creative. The Emir contributed 2.1 million Kuwaiti dinars to be the capital of the foundation. The Emir personally, in his capacity as president, appointed the members of the Supreme Council of Al-Mabarrah, which included the following: 1- Jassem Al-Sager.
2- Abdul Rahman Al-Ateeqi.
3- Dr. Abdel Mohsen Abdel Razek.
4- Abdul Latif Al-Hamad.
5- Faisal Sultan Al-Issa.
6- Mrs. Lulwa Al Qatami.
7- Dr. Ibrahim Makki as its director.

He was also selected for membership in the Board of Directors:
1- His son, Sheikh Ahmed Sabah Al-Salem Al-Sabah.
2- Mr. Nasser Al-Kharafi.
3- Mr. Suleiman Al-Hamoud Al-Khalid.
The Foundation has continued its activities since that date until now without interruption.

His achievements:
- Sheikh Sabah established four funds that were fully funded by generous Kuwaiti gifts.
- The first one:
The Kuwait Fund for Arab Economic Development in 1962 with the aim of financing economic and social projects in Arab countries.
Second:
The Arab Fund for Economic and Social Assistance is based in Kuwait, which contributes 25% of its capital, which provides all facilities without the interference of the Kuwaiti authority.
Third:
The third Kuwait Fund is the Special Economic Assistance Office for the Gulf and Yemen, which has a separate budget. The role of the Gulf and Yemen Fund has been defined in providing social welfare assistance in the areas of education, media, health and housing infrastructure.
Fourth:
The Reserve Fund for Future Generations, which came into existence in 1976 as a result of the surplus of oil revenues, whereby 10% of Kuwait’s oil revenues are transferred annually to the fund to achieve two goals, the first of which is to diversify the Kuwaiti economy, and secondly: to secure a permanent source of income for prosperity when it decreases Kuwait oil reserves.
